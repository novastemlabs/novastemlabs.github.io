One of the best places to start on a Nova STEM journey is with the 'Science Everywhere' module. This module is designed to help you explore how science affects your life each day. Scouts will learn to see science as they look around, and they will learn the fundamentals of the scientific method. This module can be completed in four (4) weeks using the plan below.

Week 1

Send out an email or note welcoming the Scouts and their adult partner to the Science Everywhere Nova module. In the note, assign the first activity - watch The Magic School Bus Rides Again - Season 1, Episode 8 - Three In One. In this episode Scouts get a lesson on water and its three basic forms--solid, liquid. When you do the experiments later, you can relate back to how things can change form (e.g. ice to water to steam) - although in the experiment in week 2 you will make a chemical reaction that produces a gas.

This activity will address 50% of Requirement #1.

Week One Agenda

Week 2

In week 2 you will host a Nova Lab (alternatively you can provide do-at-home instructions. In the Nova Lab you will guide the Scouts through using the scientific method to ask a question, create a hypothesis, experiment, observe and record, analyze and share their learning. Each Scout will use the Science Everywhere Nova Lab Worksheet for this activity.

For steps for running this activity, see Experiment: Vinegar and Baking Soda Reaction.

This addresses Requirement #3.

Week 3

In Week 3 you will complete an appropriate adventure for your Scouts rank.

For Wolf Cub Scouts the Germs Alive adventure is a fun adventure with a lot of activity and chemical reactions. In this adventure Scouts learn about germs and how they transfer from one person to another, the importance of washing hands, how germs spread when they sneeze, and how mucus works (by making slime - which is also a chemical reaction).

This addresses Requirement #2.

Week 4

In week 4 you will wrap up this module with a visit to a place where science is done - a doctors office or clinic. THis is a great place to learn how science is used in every day things, and tie it back to the adventure for Requirement #2. After the visit you can take 15 minutes to review the entire module and discuss how science affests your everyday life.

This addresses Requirement #4 and Requirement #5.



Science Everywehre Plan According to Requirements

This module is designed to help you explore how science affects your life each day. For the full requirements, see Science Everywhere on Scouting.org. The following are the requirements addressed in this Nova Stem Lab Guide.

  1. Watch an episode or episodes (about one hour total) of a show about anything related to science. Then do the following:
    1. Make a list of at least two questions or ideas from what you watched.
    2. Discuss two of the questions or ideas with your counselor.

    A good show to have the Scouts watch in Week 1 is The Magic School Bus Rides Again - Season 1, Episode 8 - Three In One. This episode focuses on the three (3) states of H2O - ice, water and steam. This is a great intro to how substances can change simply by changing something that affect them - in this case, the temperature.

    In Week 3, after the Nova Lab activity. have the Scouts watch Mythbusters Waterslide Wipeout. THis episode is an exciting one for kids, and they can see how the Mythbusters use the scientific method to bust a myth.

  2. Wolf Cub Scouts - Complete the Germs Alive! adventure. Discuss with your counselor what kind of science, technology, engineering, and math was used in the adventure or option.

    Germs Alive - Complete the following

    1. Wash your hands while singing the "germ song."
    2. Play Germ Magnet with your den or your family. Wash your hands afterward.
    3. Conduct the sneeze demonstration.
    4. Conduct the mucus demonstration with your den or family.
    5. Grow a mold culture. At a den or pack meeting, show what formed.

    Option A: Complete all of the following:

    1. Explain the scientific method to your adult partner.
    2. Use the scientific method in a simple science project. Explain the results to an adult.
    3. Talk to a scientist about why he or she became a scientist.

  3. Act like a scientist! Explore EACH of the following:
    1. With your counselor, choose a question you would like to investigate.
    2. With your counselor, use the scientific method/process to investigate your question. Keep records of your question, the information you found, how you investigated, and what you found out about your question.
      You may do 3B with another Cub Scout if you would like, but you need to do and record your own work.
    3. Discuss your investigation and findings with your counselor.
  4. Visit a place where science is being done, used, or explained.
    1. During your visit, talk to someone in charge about science.
    2. Discuss with your counselor the science done, used, or explained at the place you visited.
  5. Discuss with your counselor how science affects your everyday life.
