What Are the STEM Nova Awards?

The Boy Scouts of America developed the STEM Nova awards program to excite and expand a sense of wonder in our Scouts. By working with an adult counselor or mentor, the various modules allow them to explore the basic principles of STEM and discover how fun and fascinating STEM can be. The Supernova awards are offered for those who enjoy a super challenge. Discover how the STEM Nova Awards program helps youth be “Prepared. For Life.”

Is This for Me?

Are you an Adult Leader interested in having more STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) activities in your Pack or Den? The Nova STEM Labs were created for you. Our goal is to make it easier for you to learn how to bring STEM into your unit and discover what kind of activities you can do in each area.

What Are Nova STEM Labs?

In Nova STEM Labs we break each Nova award down into a "Lab". A Lab is simply a defined journey for a Scout to go on that aligns to one of the BSA's Nova Award modules. Each Lab is designed to be completed in 4-weeks through a combination of Adult-led demonstrations, hands-on activities and at-home assignments. Each Lab will fullfil the requirements of the associated Nova Award module.

OK, Now What?

The easiest way to get a Nova STEM program going is to explain the Nova program at a unit meeting and gauge the interest level from the Scouts. The Nova Awards are designed to be achieved by Scouts who show an interest in STEM activities - we don't recommend making it a requirment for all Scouts. The Nova STEM awards are definitely something the Scout should want to pursue.

Once you have completed the Nova Counselor or Supernova Mentor training and registered for the correct position (see below), you are welcome to freely use any of the material from this website. This website is managed and maintained as open source software and content on GitHub.com . A contributors guide is coming soon.

Each Scouting unit needs one or more Nova Counselors. This requires a commitment from the volunteer to guide Scouts on their journey, supporting them as they discover a love for STEM.

Nova Counselors

  • Must be age 21 or older
  • Register as a Nova Counselor (position code 58, non-unit position, no registration fee) using the BSA Adult Application
  • Must complete BSA’s Youth Protection Training before working with youth
  • Should complete Nova Counselor training online

Supernova Mentors

  • Must be age 21 or older
  • Must be subject matter experts in a STEM (science, technology, engineering, mathematics) field
  • Register as a Supernova Mentor (position code 52, non-unit position, no registration fee) using the BSA Adult Application
  • Must complete and submit the Supernova Awards Mentor Information form to the local council service center
  • Must complete BSA’s Youth Protection Training before working with youth
  • Should complete STEM Supernova Mentor training online
  • Supernova mentors guiding Scouts through the Dr. Albert Einstein Supernova award should reference this guide.
